Jun Seba

瀬葉 淳

Known as Nujabes (ヌジャベス, Nujabesu)

2/7/1974 - 2/26/2010

Japanese record producer, record store owner, student of hip hop

1974

Born in Nishi-Azabu, Minato, Tokyo, Japan

Jun Seba founds his independent label Hydeout Productions.

A 36-track mixtape, Sweet Sticky Thing was released on Hyde Out Recordings (a precursor to Hydeout Productions).

1998

Meets Stanley “Substantial” Robinson

1999

Jun was known for being a foodie; this is Bowery Kitchen, the curry spot that he and Substantial would visit sometimes twice a week.

Finally connects with Funky DL (left in photo) via his cell phone number that he’d put on one of his vinyl releases. DJ Sancon (middle) and Jun (right)

Luv(sic) Part 1 featuring Shing02 (Shingo Annen) is released on vinyl in Japan, which marks the first collaboration between Nujabes and Shing02.

2001

2002

The mixtape ristorante / good music cuisine: making good beats like cooking good foods is released.

Vinyl pressing of luv(sic) Part Two is released in Japan.

2003

2004

A compilation of Hydeout’s best, Hydeout Productions FIRST COLLECTION drops in April.

Metaphorical Music drops in August. This is considered a fan favorite for most.

Samurai Champloo debuted on May 19, 2004. Nujabes, along with Fat Jon, DJ Tsutchie and others collaborate on the anime’s soundtrack. Not shown are Samurai Champloo Musical Record: Masta and Samurai Champloo Musical Record: Playlist

2005

2007

Modal Soul, Jun’s second full-length album Modal Soul is released on his own label Hydeout Productions on November 11.

Luv (sic) Part 3 is released as part of Modal Soul.

What would be his last album, Hydeout Productions 2nd Collection is released in November 11.

2010

2011

At 22:14 on the night of February 26th, Jun is killed in a traffic collision upon exiting the Shuto Expressway in Shibuya.

His third and final studio album is released posthumously on December 3rd. It was put together by his close friends as it was incomplete at the time of Jun’s passing.

Luv (sic) Part 4 is released on vinyl on July 7th.

2012

2013

Luv (sic) Part 5 is released on December 26th.

Luv (sic) Part 6/ Grand Finale is released on February 26th, 3 years to the day after Jun’s passing, concluding the hexology.

“Nujabes is not dead. Just press play…”

Rest in Peace, Jun Seba.
